Process Equipment Design

PROCESS EQUIPMENT DESIGN
Code: ESC 155
Credits: 1.5
L T P: – – 3
Maximum Marks (Practical): 50
Elective: No
Contact Hours: 45

Objectives

  • To be familiar with the process and mechanical aspects of design of process equipment.
  • To understand design factors, procedures, codes, and standards.

Course Outcomes

Students should be able to apply the criteria involved in the design of:

  • Pressure vessels
  • Vessel supports
  • Various types of joints
  • Foundations as per relevant codes and specifications

List of Practicals

  1. Study of factors influencing vessel design; classification of pressure vessels, applications, methods of fabrication, fundamental principles, and equations.
  2. Study of pressure vessel codes, specifications, and standards; review of code development, ASME codes, API-ASME code, Section VIII of ASME codes.
  3. General design considerations: design pressure, design temperature, materials, design stress (nominal design strength), corrosion allowance, design loads, and minimum practical wall thickness.
  4. Design of thin-walled vessels under internal pressure: cylindrical and spherical shells, heads and closures, flat ends, domed ends, conical sections, and end closures.
  5. Design of vessels under external pressure: cylindrical shells, stiffening rings, and vessel heads.
  6. Design of vessels under combined loading: weight loads, wind loads (tall vessels), and torque.
  7. Design of welded joints and bolted flanged joints.
  8. Design of foundations and supports.
